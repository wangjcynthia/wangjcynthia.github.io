About Me

Beginning at a young age, I have fought to establish my identity as a daughter of immigrants, a first-generation student, and a queer woman of color. Through these identities, I have been able to challenge the status quo and elevate my passion for knowledge. As a leader in my community, the University of Utah, and my family, I have come to understand the impact my education and example can have upon myself and those around me. I hope to continue taking risks, giving back to my community, and challenging boundaries.

Computer science scared me. It was a mix of the enigma around what computer science is and imposter syndrome that kept me from pursuing it; I thought that coding was something that only certain people could do, and that I didn’t fit in, as computer science is a primarily white and male dominated field. But I always loved problem solving, and this innate love for it is what drew me to pursuing my degrees in Computer Science and Quantitative Analysis of Markets & Organizations.

I coded for the first time during my freshman year of college, unlike many of my peers who started coding when they started playing Roblox or Minecraft in 5th grade. Learning how to code for the first time is difficult, but even moreso when you lack proper mentorship, guidance, and resources. I often felt alienated from my peers, as I started "late" and the field is primarily White and male dominated. Luckily, I had a great peer mentor and a support system that helped assure me throughout this journey. As I continue to grow my skills and knowledge in this field, I hope to give back to my community and provide mentorship and guidance to others with marginalized identities.

In my free time, I love to spend my time being active, whether that is through climbing, working out, or doing yoga; taking care of my plant children; thrifting and flipping clothing; or making art.

Professional Experience

Microsoft | Software Engineer Intern

I worked on the Azure Compute Logic Apps team again, where this time I fully developed and implemented UI and UX for the workflow parameters experience for Logic Apps Standard in Azure Portal and VSCode, enabling users to customize their parameters and the full pipeline, overall fulfilling customer needs. I also added IntelliSense for parameters in VSCode that reacts to user input, assisting users and enhancing user experience.

Microsoft | Explore Intern

I worked on the Azure Compute Logic Apps team, where I designed and fully implemented two automated workflow templates with dynamic connection, simplifying user experience, and ultimately bringing more customers to Logic Apps. I also prepared automated workflow template service for private preview by making front-end changes and building a Github repository for users to author and submit custom templates, ultimately improving user experience and customer retention in Logic Apps.

Spatio-Temporal Isotope Analytics Lab (SPATIAL) | Undergraduate Researcher

I started working as an undergraduate researcher under Professor Gabe Bowen my freshman year. In this position, I designed and developed an app that imports and edits users’ relational data frames, allowing users to interact with the data cleaning and improving efficiency and organization of processing data prior to entry into the database. I processed and sorted large data frames utilizing R, adding new data into SPATIAL’s water isotope database for users to use. I also queried SPATIAL’s database and created R code to remove duplicate entries, reducing redundancy in the database.
